The Villager

At the edge of the Chetambe Hills, you feel like you are at the edge of the world. Looking down, you see the River Nzoia meandering through the hills to a steep vertical river bed, now known as the Nabuyole Falls. To the east, you can see the Nandi Escarpment in the neighboring county, a county known for its endless fields of tea. When the paper factory was here, a pine forest took over the westward slopes. Of course, I know this is not the etdge of Earth, but this place at the edge of my village still takes my breath away. This is Webuye in Bungoma County.

When I was last standing there, I had just been admitted to KenSAP (Kenya Scholar Access Program). I sat on the cliffs, observing daily life below me and reminiscing about how far I had come. It was the onset of the rainy season and the scent of guava trees filled the air with the fragrance of the ripe fruits. Monkeys were chattering boisterously while jumping from tree to tree. Brown mushrooms sprouted from the spongy, moist grounds. As a child, we found out the hard way which ones made for a nice delicacy or a day of stomach pains. As I walk along the hills, I see an old cargo train spewing smoke and slowly crawling toward the Uganda border. In the distance, people without money chased the train along the tracks for the chance to jump onto the sides for a free ride.

I was raised in Nabuyole Village in Webuye and lived there from my birth date 2002 to 2019 when we moved to Kikuyu. I am second in a family of 4 children and a mother.

Primary School

I started school in 2006 aged 4 at Saint Peters Academy where I did 3 years of pre school and class 1, in the 8-4-4 system. I then transferred school to Webuye AC primary School where I schooled for seven years, from 2010 to 2016 when I did my KCPE.

Secondary School

I was selected to Alliance High School join in Kikuyu after for secondary education. I joined the school in january 2017 and graduated in 2021 April.

Kenya Scholar Access Program

After graduating from Allince High School, I applied and got in to the Kenya SCholar Access Program, KenSAP, where I applied to college and got in.

Moringa School

When KenSAP asked me if I was willing to join the famous Moringa school, I quickly accepted the offer. I was extremely passionate about enrolling since this would help me be at par with other freshmen in my college who have backgrounds in IT. Since I intend to persue something in the line of Computer Technology, Moringa School and its rigorous curriculum was the best place to start. At Moringa school, I intend to harness the opportunity to learn how to learn and influence the next generation of coders. I also am ready to work hard to make coding a source of income in my future years. Moreover, staying at home for the next 6 months would be boring, and the social interaction and collaboration at Moringa school would do my social life great.
